## 6. Risk Assessment & Mitigation Strategies

### 6.1 Market Risks

**RISK 1: Competitor Price Retaliation (HIGH)**
- **Probability:** 60-70%
- **Impact:** CoLoop/Dovetail drop prices 30-40% in SEA to defend market
- **Financial Impact:** -$1.5-2M ARR (Year 3), delayed breakeven by 8-12 months
- **Mitigation:**
  1. Lock in Year 1-2 customers with 2-year contracts (20% discount incentive)
  2. Differentiate on features (code-mixing) not just price
  3. Build switching costs (integrations, data migration complexity)
  4. Focus on underserved segments competitors ignore (academics, SMBs)
- **Contingency:** Reduce CAC spend, extend runway, prioritize enterprise (higher LTV)

**RISK 2: Low Market Adoption / Longer Sales Cycles (MEDIUM)**
- **Probability:** 40-50%
- **Impact:** SEA buyers hesitant to switch from Western brands, trust deficit
- **Financial Impact:** -40% customer acquisition vs plan, 6-month delay
- **Mitigation:**
  1. Pilot programs with brand-name customers (P&G, Unilever SEA offices)
  2. Free tier for academic/NGO credibility
  3. Partnership with regional associations (ESOMAR APAC, local MR bodies)
  4. Case studies in local languages showing ROI
- **Contingency:** Pivot to agency white-label model if direct sales stall

**RISK 3: Macroeconomic Downturn in SEA (MEDIUM)**
- **Probability:** 35-45%
- **Impact:** Research budgets cut 20-30%, delayed purchasing decisions
- **Financial Impact:** -25% revenue growth, +10% churn
- **Mitigation:**
  1. ROI-focused messaging (cost savings vs manual analysis)
  2. Flexible payment terms (quarterly vs annual)
  3. Usage-based pricing option for cost-conscious buyers
  4. Expand to recession-resistant segments (government, pharma)
- **Contingency:** Reduce fixed costs 15-20%, focus on retention over acquisition

### 6.2 Competitive Risks

**RISK 4: CoLoop Acquires Local Competitor (MEDIUM-HIGH)**
- **Probability:** 50-60%
- **Impact:** Competitor gains instant SEA credibility and customer base
- **Financial Impact:** -30% market share opportunity, increased CAC
- **Mitigation:**
  1. Pre-emptive M&A: Acquire 1-2 local players ourselves (Year 2)
  2. Exclusive partnerships with key agencies
  3. Speed to market: launch 6 months ahead of projected competitor moves
  4. Patent/protect code-mixing algorithms
- **Contingency:** Differentiate on compliance/data residency (harder to replicate)

**RISK 5: New AI-Native Entrant (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic Direct Tool) (LOW-MEDIUM)**
- **Probability:** 25-35%
- **Impact:** Foundational AI companies offer free/low-cost research analysis
- **Financial Impact:** -50% pricing power, commoditization risk
- **Mitigation:**
  1. Move up the value chain: focus on workflows, collaboration, compliance (not just AI)
  2. Become the "UI layer" for AI research (integrate any LLM backend)
  3. Build proprietary features (code-mixing, SEA compliance) non-replicable by general AI
  4. Enterprise relationships and contracts as moat
- **Contingency:** Pivot to vertical integration (own transcription, become full-service)

### 6.3 Execution Risks

**RISK 6: Technical Delays / Product Quality Issues (MEDIUM)**
- **Probability:** 40-50%
- **Impact:** Backend 70% done, frontend critical; delays hurt early adopter trust
- **Financial Impact:** 3-6 month launch delay, -$500K-1M Year 1 revenue
- **Mitigation:**
  1. MVP ruthless: Ship Starter tier only first, add Enterprise features in Month 6-9
  2. Hire experienced frontend engineer (senior React/Next.js)
  3. Beta program with 20-30 forgiving early customers
  4. Comprehensive testing (Phase 8-9 focus on QA)
- **Contingency:** Launch with "Pro Services" model (we run analyses for customers) while platform matures

**RISK 7: Talent Acquisition & Retention (HIGH in SEA Tech Market)**
- **Probability:** 60-70%
- **Impact:** Singapore/Jakarta tech talent war, hard to hire/retain engineers
- **Financial Impact:** +30% salary costs, slower velocity
- **Mitigation:**
  1. Remote-first team (hire across SEA, not just SG)
  2. Equity compensation (0.5-2% for early engineers)
  3. Meaningful work narrative (building for SEA, not copying West)
  4. Flexible work, training budget, conference attendance
- **Contingency:** Offshore to Vietnam/Philippines for cost-effective engineering

**RISK 8: Data Security Breach / Compliance Failure (LOW but HIGH IMPACT)**
- **Probability:** 10-15%
- **Impact:** Breach of customer research data, regulatory fines, reputation damage
- **Financial Impact:** $500K-2M in fines/legal, 50%+ customer churn, potential shutdown
- **Mitigation:**
  1. SOC 2 Type II certification by Month 12
  2. Regular penetration testing (quarterly)
  3. Encryption at rest and in transit (standard)
  4. PDPA/GDPR compliance officer (part-time Year 1, full-time Year 2)
  5. Cyber insurance ($2M coverage)
- **Contingency:** Incident response plan, PR crisis management retainer

### 6.4 Regulatory & Compliance Risks

**RISK 9: Changing Data Localization Laws (MEDIUM)**
- **Probability:** 40-50%
- **Impact:** Indonesia, Vietnam mandate on-premise or in-country hosting only
- **Financial Impact:** +$200-400K infrastructure costs, 6-month compliance delay
- **Mitigation:**
  1. Multi-region architecture from Day 1 (SG + Jakarta + on-prem option)
  2. Partnerships with local cloud providers (Telkom Indonesia, etc.)
  3. On-premise deployment tier for government/regulated industries
  4. Active lobbying/engagement with trade associations
