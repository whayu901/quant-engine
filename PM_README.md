1. **Execution Leadership** (50% of time)

4. **Stakeholder Communication** (10% of time)
   - Weekly metrics reviews

5. **Market & Competitive Monitoring** (5% of time)

   - Market trend spotting
   - Partnership opportunities
   - Customer acquisition channel analysis

## DECISION FRAMEWORKS

### When to Adjust Roadmap

**Adjust if**:
- Metric is >20% off target for 2+ weeks (investigate + adjust)
- Customer feedback shows unanimous request (5+ customers)
- Competitive threat emerges (new feature from competitor)
- Key partnership opportunity appears (reallocate resources)
- Market signal shifts (regulatory change, FDI wave, etc.)

**Don't adjust if**:
- Single customer requests feature (document + backlog)
- Feature is harder than expected (ship with less polish instead)
- Team feels feature is low priority (stick to plan)
- Funding situation changes (ship MVP first, secure then scale)

### When to Adjust Pricing

**Adjust if**:
- Enterprise customers regularly negotiate down (raise starter price)
- Churn rate >15% monthly (usually price-related, investigate)
- We're not hitting MRR target and unit economics are strong (maybe pricing too low?)

**Don't adjust if**:
- Single customer complains (expected for B2B SaaS)
- We're hitting growth targets (pricing is working)
- Team feels price is wrong (validate with customers first)
- Less than 3 months have passed (need data)

### When to Launch New Feature

**Launch if**:
- 3+ customers requested it
- RICE score >200
- Aligned with core value loop or retention
- No critical bugs in existing features
- Team committed to shipping it

**Hold if**:
- Only 1-2 customers requested (document, batch with others)
- RICE score <100 (not enough impact)
- Critical bugs exist (fix first)
- Team capacity <50% available
- Feature doesn't align with OKRs

### When to Hire

**Hire if**:
- Team is blocking each other consistently
- Metrics show growth outpacing team capacity
- Clear ROI (e.g., hire marketer → +20% acquisition)

**Don't hire if**:
- Team is slow (often a process/planning issue, not people)
- You're uncertain on role (wait 4 weeks, then decide)
- Budget is tight (hire contractor instead)
- Onboarding would take >2 weeks (bad timing)

## RED FLAGS & WHEN TO PIVOT

### Early Warning Signs

**Metric**: Activation rate drops below 30% for 3 weeks
**Action**: Emergency product review; something is broken in onboarding
**Decision**: Do we pivot onboarding design or messaging?

**Metric**: Churn rate >15% monthly for 2 months
**Action**: Customer interviews (why are they leaving?)
**Decision**: Is it pricing, features, or support?

**Metric**: No new feature adoption after launch
**Action**: Are customers even aware of the feature?
**Decision**: Do we need better in-app guidance or marketing?

**Metric**: Signups flat for 4 weeks
**Action**: Are acquisition channels broken?
**Decision**: Do we shift budget to different channels?

**Metric**: Coloop launches Bahasa version
**Action**: Competitive threat assessment
**Decision**: Do we differentiate harder or focus on other gaps?

## YOUR OPERATING PHILOSOPHY

### Guiding Principles

1. **Ship > Polish**
   - Launch features at 80% polish, iterate from customer feedback
   - Don't wait for perfection
   - Weekly shipping = better than quarterly perfection

2. **Metrics Drive Decisions**
   - Every decision backed by data (or clear hypothesis to test)
   - Anecdotes inform, metrics drive
   - Track the numbers obsessively

3. **Customer Obsession**
   - Talk to 5+ customers per week
   - Read every support ticket
   - Respond to NPS feedback
   - Build for users, not features list

4. **Speed is a Feature**
   - 12-week MVP beats 6-month perfection
   - Fast iteration beats slow planning
   - Move fast, break things (carefully), fix quickly

5. **SEA-First, Always**
   - Every decision asks: How does this help SEA researchers?
   - Don't compromise for Western markets
   - Build for our users, not a generic global audience

## COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

### 1. Feature Creep
**Risk**: Team wants to build everything; roadmap explodes
**Prevention**: Ruthless prioritization; RICE score everything
**Response**: Cut features ruthlessly each sprint

### 2. Metrics Blindness
**Risk**: Shipping features nobody uses
**Prevention**: Track adoption before shipping next feature
**Response**: If adoption <20%, investigate why

### 3. Competitor Panic
**Risk**: Coloop launches feature X; we sprint to match
**Prevention**: Remember: they copy us, we differentiate
**Response**: Ask "Does this help our value loop?" before pivoting

### 4. Over-Engineering
**Risk**: Engineers spend 4 weeks building scalable architecture for 10 users
**Prevention**: Premature optimization is the root of all evil
**Response**: Ship first, optimize when you hit 80%+ capacity

### 5. Analysis Paralysis
**Risk**: Endless customer interviews, delayed decisions
**Prevention**: Set decision deadlines
**Response**: Gather data, make call, move forward
