## Experiment 1A: Language Barrier Pain Interviews
**Goal:** Validate that SEA researchers struggle with English-only qual tools.
**Timeline:** Days 1-7
**Budget:** $0-500

### Recruitment Script (LinkedIn/Email)

```

handle transcript analysis, especially for local-language interviews.

Would you have 10-15 minutes this week for a quick call? I'd love to hear about:
- Your current workflow from recording → report
- Tools you use (if any)
- Pain points in the process

Happy to send a [Starbucks/local coffee shop] voucher as thanks for your time!

Best,
[Your name]

P.S. This is research, not a sales call. I'm exploring whether there's a better way
to handle code-mixed transcripts (Bahasa-English, Taglish, etc.).
```

### Interview Script (15 minutes)

**Introduction (2 min)**
```
Thank you for your time! I'm exploring how researchers handle qualitative analysis
in SEA, specifically around language challenges.

Quick background: What's your role? How many qual projects do you handle per year?

[Listen, don't pitch.]
```

**Part 1: Workflow Understanding (5 min)**
```
1. "Walk me through your last qualitative project, from recording to final report."
   [Listen for: Do they transcribe? Who does it? What tools?]

2. "What language was the interview in?"
   [Listen for: Pure English? Pure local? Code-mixed?]

3. "How did you handle transcription and analysis?"
   [Listen for: Manual? Tools? Outsourced?]

4. "What tools do you currently use for qual analysis, if any?"
   [Listen for: Excel? Dovetail? NVivo? Manual? Agency in-house tool?]
```

**Part 2: Pain Point Discovery (5 min)**
```
5. "What's the most frustrating part of the analysis process?"
   [Listen for: Language? Time? Cost? Quality? Reliability?]

6. "Have you tried any international qual tools (like Dovetail, coloop, Notably)?"
   [If YES: "What was your experience? Did language come up?"]
   [If NO: "Why not?"]

7. "When you have Indonesian/Thai/Tagalog transcripts, how do you analyze them?"
   [Listen for: Translate first? Analyze in original? Code-mixing issues?]

8. "How big a problem is code-mixing (e.g., 'Service-nya bagus sekali') in your work?"
   [Listen for: Real daily issue? Occasional annoyance? Not an issue?]
```

**Part 3: Solution Validation (3 min)**
```
9. "If there was a tool that could transcribe and analyze code-mixed transcripts
    automatically, how valuable would that be?"
    [Scale 1-10, then ask WHY that score]

10. "What would make you switch from your current process to a new tool?"
    [Listen for: Time savings? Cost? Accuracy? Ease of use?]

11. "Roughly, what's your monthly budget for qual research tools per person?"
    [Listen for: $0? $50? $100? $500? "We don't buy tools"?]
```

**Closing**
```
This is super helpful, thank you!

One last thing: Would you be open to trying an early version of a SEA-focused
qual tool when it's ready? No obligation, just want to make sure we build
something useful.

[If YES: Get email and send follow-up]
```

### Manual Processing Workflow (You do this)

**Step 1: Transcription (if needed)**
- If audio/video: Use Deepgram or manual transcription
- Time: 1-2 hours per transcript

**Step 2: Run through your backend**
```bash
# Use your existing backend API
curl -X POST http://localhost:8000/api/v1/transcripts \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN" \
  -d '{"content": "transcript text here", "language": "id"}'

  -d '{"transcript_id": "xxx"}'
```

### Gate 1: After Experiment 1A + 1B + 1C (Day 14)

**GO Decision (proceed to Concierge MVP):**
- ✅ Language pain confirmed by 7+ researchers
- ✅ No strong local competitors found
- ✅ Pricing validated ($49+ acceptable to 30%+)

- ⚠️ Language is a pain, but not top-3 → Pivot positioning to speed/ease, not language
- ⚠️ Pricing too high → Test $29 tier or freemium model

**STOP Decision:**
- ❌ Language is NOT a pain → Market doesn't exist

- ❌ No one will pay >$20/month → Unit economics broken

### Gate 2: After Experiment 2A (Day 28)

**GO Decision (build frontend MVP):**
- ✅ 3+ beta users would use output in client reports
- ✅ Average trust score >7
- ✅ Time savings >2 hours per transcript
- ✅ WTP >$40/month

**ITERATE Decision:**
- ⚠️ Trust score 5-7 → Add human-in-loop, explainability features
- ⚠️ Format issues → Adjust output to match their reporting templates
- ⚠️ Feature gaps → Prioritize missing features before general build

**PIVOT Decision:**
- ❌ Trust score <5 → Pivot to human-assisted coding, not AI autopilot
- ❌ "Would never use in reports" → Pivot to internal analysis tool, not client-facing
- ❌ WTP <$30 → Pivot to agency white-label or service model

## FAQ

**Q: What if I can't get 10 interviews?**
A: Get 5 minimum. Quality > quantity. But if you can't find 5 people willing to talk about this problem, that's a signal the market isn't there.

**Q: What if competitors exist but are weak?**
A: That's actually good validation (market exists, but underserved). Proceed, but differentiate clearly.

**Q: What if pricing test shows everyone wants free?**
A: Test a $29 tier. If that also fails, pivot to freemium or per-project pricing. If no one will pay anything, you have a hobby, not a business.

**Q: What if the concierge MVP users love it but won't pay?**
A: Ask why. Might be:
- Budget constraints (target different segment)
- Perception of value (communicate ROI better)
- Prefer done-for-you service (pivot to service model)

**Q: How do I know when to stop vs pivot?**
A: Stop if: no one has the pain you're solving, or they have the pain but won't pay enough to cover costs.
Pivot if: pain exists, but your solution/pricing/positioning is wrong.
