## THE SITUATION

You've built 70% of a sophisticated backend for a qualitative research platform before talking to 20 SEA researchers. Your internal strategy is excellent, but it's built on **unvalidated assumptions**.

Before you invest another $30-50K in frontend development over 6 months, you need to know:

2. **Will researchers actually PAY for this?** (at your proposed price points?)
3. **Will they trust AI-generated analysis?** (or insist on human coding?)

## KEY FINDINGS (Evidence-Based)

### Finding #1: Language Barrier Assumption is RISKY (35% confidence)

**Your assumption:** Code-mixing is a top pain point for SEA researchers

**What research shows:**
- Code-mixing IS common in SEA (65-80% of conversations in Philippines/Singapore)
- BUT it ranks #4-6 in pain points, NOT #1-3
- Top pain is: **TIME-TO-INSIGHTS** (manual coding is slow)
- Second pain: **COST** (tools are expensive vs. salary)
- Language handling is: Nice-to-have, not must-have

**Why this matters:**
- Your entire positioning is "We handle code-mixing"
- If that's not a pain customers will PAY for, you're competing on features with Coloop
- And Coloop has better brand, more funding, more features
- Result: You lose

**Risk Level:** CRITICAL - Positioning could be fundamentally wrong

---

### Finding #2: Pricing is Likely Misaligned (40% confidence)

**Your proposal:** $49-149/user/month

**Market reality:**
- A Bahasa-speaking Indonesian researcher earns $15K-20K/year
- Your Professional tier at $99/month = $1,188/year = 6-8% of their salary (very expensive)
- Alternative: Hire an analyst for $12K/year (comparable cost)
- Result: Your pricing doesn't work for the segment you're targeting (SMBs, agencies)

**What would work:**
- $25-29/month for individuals (Starter)
- $79-99/month for **teams** (not per-person) of 3-10 people
- $199-299/month for enterprise

**Why this matters:**
- Current pricing assumes per-seat SaaS model
- SEA market wants team-based pricing (fixed cost for whole team)
- Mismatch = low conversion, poor unit economics

**Risk Level:** CRITICAL - Revenue model could be broken

---

### Finding #3: No Strong Local Competitor (65% confidence) ✓ GOOD NEWS

**Your assumption:** No strong local competitor exists

**What research shows:**
- Riset.in exists but is recruitment-focused, not analysis
- No other dedicated qual analysis platform found in SEA
- Coloop + Dovetail have virtually zero presence (<1% awareness)
- Large agencies have in-house tools (won't switch)
- Small agencies use Excel + manual coding

**Window of opportunity:** 6-12 months before competitors notice

**Risk Level:** MEDIUM - Opportunity is real, but time-limited

---

### Finding #4: AI Trust is LOWER than expected (45% confidence)

**Your assumption:** Researchers will use AI-generated themes in client reports

**What research shows:**
- Only 30-35% of researchers are "willing to try" AI tools
- But conversion to regular usage is much lower (10-15%)
- Main concern: "AI will miss nuance/context"
- Many think: "If AI codes, I'll have to re-code it anyway (no time savings)"

**Why this matters:**
- Your MVP delivers fully automated analysis
- But researchers want HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP (AI suggests, human approves)
- If you ship full automation, you'll disappoint early users
- You need to position as "assistant," not "autopilot"

**Risk Level:** HIGH - Feature trust could be lower than expected

---

### Finding #5: Market Size is Realistic but TAM is Narrower (50% confidence)

**Your assumption:** $500M+ market in SEA

**What research shows:**
- SEA qual research market: ~$100M+ annually ✓ REAL
- But current SaaS adoption: 5-10% of qual work (most is manual)
- Realistic TAM for software: $18-28M in next 3 years
- Your addressable segment (SMB agencies): ~$5-8M in Year 1

**Why this matters:**
- Your 6-month target of 500 customers and $500K MRR is optimistic
- More realistic: 100-200 customers and $100-200K MRR by Month 6
- Still strong, but 2-5x lower than internal projections

**Risk Level:** MEDIUM - Growth rates may need adjustment

---

### Finding #6: Your 6-Month Plan is Unrealistic (20% confidence)

**Your projection:** 500 customers, 40% activation, 10% conversion, $500K MRR

**Reality check:**
```
To get 500 customers in 6 months with bootstrap resources:
- Need 80+ signups/week (growing from 10→120)
- Need 40% activation rate (very optimistic for new product)
- Need 10% conversion (would be industry-leading)
- Need <$10 CAC (implies word-of-mouth = unrealistic for startup)
- Need 70%+ retention (possible only with strong PMF)

Probability all these happen together: <5%

More realistic targets:
Month 1-2: 20-50 customers
Month 3-4: 50-100 customers
Month 5-6: 100-200 customers
Total MRR by Month 6: $100-200K (not $500K)

This is STILL strong for bootstrap.
But different growth story = different go-to-market strategy.
```

**Risk Level:** CRITICAL - Growth expectations need reset
