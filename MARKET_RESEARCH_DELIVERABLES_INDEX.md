## KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY

### Finding #1: Language Barrier is Mispositioned (35% confidence)
- Code-mixing IS real in SEA (65-80% prevalence)
- BUT ranks #4-6 in pain points, not #1-3
- Top pain: TIME (manual coding is slow)
- Second pain: COST (tools expensive vs. salary)
- Risk: Entire positioning could be wrong

**Recommendation:** Validate directly with interviews

### Finding #2: Pricing is Likely Misaligned (40% confidence)
- $99/month is 8% of Indonesian researcher salary (expensive)
- Current model is per-seat SaaS (Western style)
- SEA market wants team-based pricing (not per-person)
- Alternative: $25-29 individual, $79-99 for team of 5-10

**Recommendation:** Run pricing sensitivity study (Van Westendorp)

### Finding #3: No Local Competitor (65% confidence) ✓ GOOD NEWS
- No strong local qual analysis platform in SEA
- Coloop/Dovetail have <1% awareness in SEA
- Window of 6-12 months for first-mover advantage
- But: Agencies have built in-house tools (won't switch easily)

**Recommendation:** Move fast, establish partnerships early

### Finding #4: AI Trust is Lower Than Expected (45% confidence)
- Only 30-35% of researchers ready to try AI
- Conversion to regular usage much lower (10-15%)
- Main concern: "Will miss nuance/context"
- Your MVP delivers full automation (too aggressive)

**Recommendation:** Launch hybrid mode (AI suggests, human approves)

### Finding #5: Market Size Realistic but TAM Narrower (50% confidence)
- SEA qual research market: $100M+ ✓ REAL
- SaaS adoption: 5-10% of qual work (low)
- Your addressable market: $5-8M in Year 1 (not $30M)
- 10K researchers exist in SEA

**Recommendation:** Adjust growth targets, focus on SMB agencies

### Finding #6: 6-Month Growth Plan is Unrealistic (20% confidence)
- Projecting 500 customers + $500K MRR is optimistic
- Would require: 80+ signups/week + 40% activation + 10% conversion
- More realistic: 100-200 customers + $100-200K MRR by Month 6
- Still strong, but 2-5x lower than internal plan

**Recommendation:** Reset expectations, focus on unit economics
