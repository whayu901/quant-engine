## Market Gaps (Why Now?)

### 1. **Pricing Gap**
- Western tools: USD 300-600/user/month
- SEA affordability threshold: USD 50-150/user/month
- **Opportunity**: 80% of SEA teams can't afford Coloop

### 2. **Language Gap**
- No tool handles code-mixing (Taglish, Singlish, bahasa gaul)
- Western tools force English translation (loses nuance)
- **Opportunity**: Local language = better insights

### 3. **Compliance Gap**
- International tools = US data centers only
- Regulation tightening: Indonesia (UU PDP), Thailand/Singapore (PDPA), Vietnam (data localization)
- **Opportunity**: In-region hosting mandatory for enterprise

### 4. **Mobile Gap**
- 85% of SEA internet is mobile; Western tools assume desktop
- Field researchers need phone-first workflows
- **Opportunity**: Mobile upload + async analysis

### 5. **Support Gap**
- International tools = support during US business hours
- SEA teams need local support, prefer local language
- **Opportunity**: Local engineering + responsive support

### 6. **Integration Gap**
- No international tool integrates with WhatsApp, SurveyToGo, regional platforms
- **Opportunity**: Local ecosystem integrations

1. **Regulatory pressure** — PDPA enforcement ramping up

# COMPETITIVE STRATEGY & PLAYBOOK

## How We Win vs. Coloop.ai (Main Threat)

### **Phase 1: Establish Beachhead (Months 1-3)**

**Coloop's Slow Response Curve**:
- Coloop needs 6+ months to launch SEA language support
- 3-4 months for regional pricing strategy
- 2-3 months for compliance review
- We have 12-month first-mover advantage

**Our playbook**:
```
Month 1: Launch MVP in Singapore (English)
  → 300+ signups, 40+ paid
  → Establish credibility

Month 2: Launch Bahasa Indonesia version
  → 200+ signups, 10+ paid
  → First "They get us" moment for SEA market

Month 3: Have 400+ customers across SG + ID
  → 10% of Coloop's global base, but highly concentrated in SEA
  → By time Coloop reacts (Month 6-7), we're already at 800+ customers
```

---

### **Phase 2: Build Moats (Months 4-9)**

**1. Code-Mixing Database Moat**
- As we analyze more SEA transcripts, our code-mixing detection improves
- We collect training data that competitors don't have access to
- By Month 6, we've analyzed 1,000+ code-mixed transcripts
- By Month 12, we've analyzed 10,000+ — our model is SEA-specific
- This moat is **very hard to replicate** (requires SEA data)

**Action**: Train proprietary models on anonymized transcript data

**2. Local Compliance Moat**
- We have in-region data centers (Singapore, Jakarta) from Month 1
- Coloop will take 6+ months to establish data residency
- By that time, enterprises have already moved to us for compliance
- Switching cost is high (data residency is critical)

**Action**: Partner with local cloud providers (AWS SG, GCP Singapore)

**3. Community & Ecosystem Moat**
- Build SEA researcher community (WhatsApp groups, LinkedIn, associations)
- Integrate with local tools (SurveyToGo, regional payment methods)
- By Month 6, we have 20+ partnerships vs. Coloop's 0
- Ecosystem is hard to build; creates stickiness

**Action**: 4-5 partnership deals per month (Months 2-6)

**4. Team Stickiness Moat**
- Multi-seat adoption is key (team adoption = higher switching cost)
- Our lower pricing enables teams to adopt (all 3 researchers on team can have seats)
- Coloop's pricing means only team lead has seat
- By Month 3, 30% of our customers have 3+ team members
- Team dynamics = hard to switch

**Action**: Focus on multi-seat adoption in sales/marketing

---

### **Phase 3: Defend & Expand (Months 10-18)**

**If Coloop launches competitive SEA product** (Month 7-9):

1. **Price stay lower** — We maintain 30-40% pricing advantage
2. **Language stay better** — Our code-mixing is more mature (more data)
3. **Compliance stay stronger** — Our local expertise (already hired local teams)
4. **Community stay larger** — We have 2,000+ users vs. their 100+ (head start)
5. **Features stay equal** — We match their new features within 4-6 weeks

**Competitive response playbook**:
```
Week 1-2: Announce we're adding Coloop's new features
  → Blog post: "We'll do this in 4 weeks, better than Coloop"

Week 3-4: Develop new feature

Week 5: Launch feature + marketing
  → "Qual Engine adds [feature] — cheaper + more localized than Coloop"

Customer impact:
```
