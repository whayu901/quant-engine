IN-DEPTH INTERVIEW - FOOD DELIVERY APP USAGE
Date: 22 June 2024
Location: Singapore
Participant: Professional, Age 29

---

INTERVIEWER: Thanks for joining us today. Can you tell me about your food delivery app usage?

PARTICIPANT: Sure! I use food delivery apps almost daily actually. Mainly GrabFood and Foodpanda, sometimes Deliveroo for specific restaurants. During weekdays, definitely lunch everyday because no time to go out from office. Dinner maybe 3-4 times a week when I work late.

INTERVIEWER: That's quite frequent. What drives your decision to order instead of cooking or eating out?

PARTICIPANT: Time is the biggest factor lah. My work schedule very hectic, sometimes back-to-back meetings until 8pm. By the time reach home, too tired to cook already. Also, living alone, cooking for one person not very economical. Buy ingredients, use a bit then throw away when spoil. Food delivery more practical for my lifestyle.

INTERVIEWER: How do you choose between different delivery platforms?

PARTICIPANT: Honestly ah, I check all three apps before ordering! Compare delivery fees, promo codes, estimated time. GrabFood usually faster because more riders, but Foodpanda sometimes got better deals. Deliveroo has some exclusive restaurants that others don't have. I'm very price conscious despite ordering frequently - every dollar counts!

The subscription programs quite worth it if you order often. I have GrabUnlimited and Pandapro. The free delivery pays for itself within first week of the month. But must be disciplined lah, cannot anyhow order just because got free delivery.

INTERVIEWER: What about your experience with the service quality?

PARTICIPANT: Generally okay, but got pain points. Biggest issue is when food arrives cold or soggy. Especially fried items, by the time reach already not crispy. Some restaurants don't pack properly for delivery - soup spill everywhere, or items missing.

Customer service very important when things go wrong. Grab usually quite responsive, can get refund quickly through app. Foodpanda sometimes take longer to resolve. But I understand riders also under pressure, especially during peak hours or rain.

The rating system quite stressful actually. Feel bad to give low rating because affect their livelihood, but sometimes service really CMI [cannot make it]. Usually I only rate if very good or very bad, otherwise just skip.

INTERVIEWER: Tell me about any memorable experiences, good or bad?

PARTICIPANT: Got one time, ordered dinner for important virtual meeting with overseas clients. The delivery delayed by one hour! App showed rider at restaurant for 45 minutes. Super stressed, keep refreshing app. End up had to eat cup noodles during the call. After that, I always order buffer time for important occasions.

But also got good experience. Once ordered when sick with fever, put in notes asking rider to leave at door and knock. The rider actually WhatsApp ask if I need anything from pharmacy since he passing by! So sweet. I gave big tip through app.

INTERVIEWER: How has your usage pattern changed over time?

PARTICIPANT: Pandemic completely changed my habits. Before COVID, maybe order once a week only, usually weekend treat. During circuit breaker, became daily necessity. Now hybrid working, so usage still high but more strategic.

I also more adventurous now with trying new cuisines. The apps make it easy to discover new restaurants. Last time only order from places I know, now willing to try based on ratings and reviews. The photos from other customers very helpful.

Price sensitivity increased though. Last time don't really check, just order. Now with inflation, more careful about delivery fees and mark-up prices. Sometimes I order direct from restaurant if they have their own delivery to save money.

INTERVIEWER: What features do you wish these apps had?

PARTICIPANT: Group order function can be better. Now very leceh [troublesome] to coordinate with colleagues. Would be good to have split bill feature, shared cart, everyone can add their items and pay separately.

Meal planning feature would be useful. Like, let me schedule whole week of meals in advance, then auto-order at specified times. Good for busy periods when I know won't have time to think about food.

Better dietary filters needed. I try to eat healthy, but very hard to find nutritional info. Would be good to filter by calories, protein content, etc. Also allergen information not always clear.

INTERVIEWER: How do you see food delivery evolving in Singapore?

PARTICIPANT: Confirm will become even more integrated into daily life. Maybe drone delivery in future? HDB already testing. That would solve the manpower issue and maybe reduce cost.

Sustainability is important. The amount of plastic waste from packaging quite jialat [terrible]. Hope to see more eco-friendly packaging, maybe returnable containers system like dabao [takeaway] in the past.

Robot delivery already starting in some areas. Quite cool but wonder how practical for high-rise buildings. The human touch also important - riders often help elderly or disabled customers.

INTERVIEWER: Any other thoughts about the food delivery ecosystem?

PARTICIPANT: The gig economy aspect quite concerning. Riders work so hard but no job security, no CPF, no medical benefits. As frequent user, feel guilty sometimes. Try to tip when can afford, especially during bad weather.

Dark kitchens trending now - restaurants that only do delivery, no dine-in. Makes sense business-wise, but wonder about food quality and hygiene standards. Prefer ordering from proper restaurants that I can visit if needed.

The data these apps collect quite scary if you think about it. They know my eating habits better than myself! But convenient lah, the personalized recommendations quite accurate. Just hope they protect the data properly.

INTERVIEWER: Finally, would you say you're dependent on food delivery apps?

PARTICIPANT: Wah, this one hard question. I would say... yes, quite dependent already. If all apps down, will struggle especially on weekdays. It's become such an integral part of my routine. Both blessing and curse lah.

But trying to be more mindful. Set monthly budget for food delivery, try to cook on weekends, and support hawker centres when possible. Balance is important. These apps are tools for convenience, shouldn't let them control our lives completely.

The social aspect of eating also important. Sometimes order delivery even when friends gathering because so used to it already. Must remember that sharing meal together, without screen, also got its value. Food is not just about filling stomach, it's about connection and culture too.

INTERVIEWER: That's a very thoughtful perspective. Thank you so much for sharing your experiences!

PARTICIPANT: No problem! Hope it helps. Actually quite therapeutic to reflect on my delivery app usage. Maybe I should really cut down a bit haha!

[END OF TRANSCRIPT]
